Alton Brown Turkey Recipe

Description

This is the recipe from the show "Good Eats - Thanksgiving Special."
It is a very easy recipe to complete and I have been using it for over 17 years.

The total time is 9 hours and 45 minutes.
The prep time is 15 minutes.
The cooking time is 2 hours and 30 minutes for a 14 to 16 pound turkey.
This will yeild around 10 to 12 servings.

Ingredients:

Main

  • 1 (14 to 16 pound) frozen young turkey
  • 5 gallon bucket

For the Brine

  • 1 cup kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 gallon vegetable stock
  • 1 tablespoon black perrercorns
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons allspice berries
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons chopped candied ginger
  • 1 gallon hevily iced water

For the Aromatics

  • 1 red apple, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 cup water
  • 4 sprigs rosemary
  • 6 leaves sage
  • Canola Oil

Directions

  1. Two to three days before roasting: Begin
    thawing the turkey in the refrigerator or in a
    cooler kept at 38 degrees F.

  2. Combine the vegetable stock, salt, brown
    sugar, peppercorns, allspice berries,
    and candied ginger in a large stockpot over medium-
    high heat. Stir occasionally to dissolve solids around
    bring to a boil. Then remove the brine from the
    heat, cool to room tempature, and refrigerate.

  3. The night before or early on the day you'd like
    to eat: Combine the brine, water and ice in
    the 5-gallon bucket. Place the thawed turkey (with
    innards removed) breast side down in brince. If
    necessary, weigh downthe bird to ensure it is fully
    immersed, cover, and refregerate or set in cool area
    for 8 to 16 hours, turning the bird once half way
    through bringing.

  4. Preheat the oven to 500 degrees F. remove
    the fird from brine and rinse inside and out
    with cold water. Discard the brine.

  5. Place the bird on roasting rack inside a half
    sheet pan and pat dry with paper towel.

  6. Combine the apple, onion, cinnamon stick,
    and 1 cup of water in a microwave safe dish
    and microwave on high for 5 minutes. Add steeped
    aromatics to the turkey's cavity along with the
    rosemary and sage. Tuck the wings underneath the
    bird and coat the skin liberally with canola oil.

  7. Roast the turkey on lowest level of the oven
    at 500 degrees F for 30 minutes. Insert a
    probe thermometer into the thickest part of the breast
    and reduce the oven tempature to 350 degrees
    F. Set the thermometer alarm (if available) to 161
    degrees F. A 14 to 16 pound bird should require a
    total of 2 to 2 1/2 hours of roasting. Let the turkey
    rest, loosely covered with foil or a large mixing bowl
    for 15 minutes before carving.
