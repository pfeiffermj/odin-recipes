Alton Brown City Ham

Description

This is the recipe from the show "Good Eats - Ham I Am."
It is a very easy recipe to complete and I will be using it for
Christmas 2022.

The total time is 6 hours and 15 minutes.
The prep time is 15 minutes.
The cooking time is 6 hours.
This will yeild around 10 to 15 servings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 city style (brined) ham, hock end
  • 1/4 cup brown mustard
  • 2 cups dark brown sugar
  • 1 ounce bourbon (poured into a spritz bottle
  • 2 cups crushed ginger snap cookies

Directions

  1. Heat oven to 250 degrees F.

  2. Remove ham from bag, rinse and drain
    thoroughly. Place ham, cut side down, in a
    roasting pan. Using a small pairing knife or clean
    utility knife set to the smallest blade setting, score
    the ham from bottom to top, spiraling clockwise as
    you cut.(If you're using a pairing knife, be careful to
    only cut through the skin and first few layers of fat).
    Rotate the ham after each cut so that the scores
    are no more than 2-inches across. Once you've
    made it all the way around, move the knife to the
    other hand and repeat, spiraling counter clockwise.
    The aim is to create a diamond pattern all over the
    ham. (Don't worry too much about precision here.)

  3. Tent the ham with heavy duty foil, insert a
    thermometer, and cook for 3 to 4 hours or
    until the internal tempature at the deepest part
    of the meat registers 130 degrees F.

  4. Remove and use tongs to pull away the
    diamonds of skin and any sheets of fat that
    come off with them.

  5. Heat the ove to 350 degrees F.

  6. Dab dry with paper towels, then brush on a
    liberal coat of mustard, using either a
    basting brush or a clean paint brush. (clean as in
    never-touched paint). Sprinkle on brown sugar
    packing loosely as you go until the hame is coated.
    Spritz this layer lightly with bourbon, then looslely
    pack on as much of the crushed cookies as you can.

  7. Insert the thermometer (don't use he old
    hole) and return to the oven (uncovered).
    Cook until interior tempature reaches 140
    degrees F, approximately 1 hour.

  8. Let the roast rest for 1/2 hour before carving.

  9. Cook's note: A city ham is basically any
    brined ham that's packed is a plastic bag,
    held in a refrigerated case and marked "ready to
    cook","Partially cooked" or "ready to server". Better
    city hams are also labeled "ham in natural juices"
